The battalion joined the people of Naltoqa in celebrating Tuluni, the festival of the Sumi Naga community that marks the season of plenty. Families, personnel, and guests from neighbouring villages gathered on the battalion grounds for a day of shared food, song, and gratitude. The morning opened with a traditional invocation, followed by folk songs and dances performed by cultural troupes from the surrounding area. Children from the battalion school presented a short programme of their own, earning the loudest applause of the day.

As is the custom of Tuluni, the celebration centred on the feast. Long tables were laid out in the open, and the community ate together — a reminder that the battalion's story and the village's story are one and the same.
